一

Auspicious Wishes: Ruyi Sceptres in the Palace Museum Collection

The exhibition “ Auspicious Wishes: Ruyi Sceptres in the Palace Museum Collection” opened on 10 February 2021 at the Gate of Divine Prowess (Shenwu men) in the Forbidden City as the Palace Museum’s first exhibition of the Chinese New Year. The featured 160 pieces (or sets) are among the Palace Museum’s finest ruyi sceptres as chosen from the collection of over 2,000 related works. The exhibition is divided into the following five sections: Jade Ruyi Sceptres, Bamboo and Wood Ruyi Sceptres, Metal Ruyi Sceptres, Ninefold Ruyi Sceptres, and Ruyi Sceptres of Various Materials. The displays comprehensively reflect the abundant materials, beautiful designs, refined craftsmanship, and numerous themes of ruyi sceptres of the Ming (1368–1644) and Qing (1644–1911) dynasties. Walking through the galleries, visitors will find a dazzling array of works while, outside the halls atop the Gate of Divine Prowess, stunning scenery affords viewers with a relaxing atmosphere to calm the body and mind.

二

Lingering Voice of the Sages: The Culture of Qin

The Palace Museum was established on the site of the Ming (1368-1644) and the Qing (1644-1911) imperial palace and thus inherited the rich court collection; since the establishment of the museum, the collection of qin has further grown. Consequently, it can now claim to house the world’s finest collection of qin. The Palace Museum has a qin collection of eighty-eight pieces from the Tang, the Song (960-1279), the Yuan (1272-1368), the Ming, and the Qing dynasties, forming a complete series of fine instruments across time and thus giving a relatively complete picture of the instrument's development. This exhibition displays various kinds of related materials, giving visitors a view of qin culture from all angles.

三

The Way of Construction and Preservation of the Forbidden City

The special exhibition consists of four units:“ Planning and Design ”, “ Interior Decoration and Design ”,“ Tilework and Design ”“ Colored Painting and Design ”.This gate tower exhibition showcases exquisite architectural components, pressed models by Chinese Architect Family Lei, and design drawings; a pathway to the rooftop is also built to provide a close-up view of the structural components and colored paintings. Meanwhile, the city wall between the Meridian Gate and the East Prosperity Gate is also open to the public, offering a fabulous aesthetic experience of the architecture; the southeast corner watchtower is now accessible, so is the beauty of its brilliant structure, which is also brought to view via digital production The Corner Watchtower.
