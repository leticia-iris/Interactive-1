My dear Theo, I’m sending you herewith a small drawing. I made it last Sunday, the morning a daughter (13 years old) of my landlady died. It’s a view of Streatham Common, a large, grass-covered area with oak trees and broom. It had rained in the night, and the ground was soggy here and there and the young spring grass fresh and green Sad and alone, I climbed the sad, bare dune, Where the sea keens its ceaseless moaning plaint, The dune where dies the wide unfurling wave, Drab path that winds and winds upon itself again.
